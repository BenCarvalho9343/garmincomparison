Pros & Cons

Edge Explore 2

The Navigation Specialist

Pros

  • Large, bright 3" touchscreen — easiest to read at a glance
  • Full onboard maps with auto-rerouting
  • Intuitive UI — nearly zero learning curve
  • Points of interest for multi-day touring
  • Group LiveTrack for group rides
  • Varia radar & smart light compatible
  • ClimbPro for elevation anticipation

Cons

  • Shorter battery life (16 h) than Edge 530
  • Heavier and bulkier than the other two
  • Limited advanced training features
  • No electronic shifting integration
  • Touchscreen less reliable in heavy rain or gloves
  • Overkill for pure training use

Edge 530

The Performance Powerhouse

Pros

  • Best-in-class training metrics (TSS, NP, IF)
  • Full Training Status & recovery guidance
  • Longest battery life at 20 hours
  • Di2/eTap electronic shifting integration
  • MTB-specific dynamics (jumps, grit, flow)
  • Extensive Connect IQ app ecosystem
  • Physical buttons — works in any weather, with gloves
  • Full ClimbPro & mapping support

Cons

  • Older 2019 hardware (though still well-supported)
  • Smaller screen than Explore 2
  • Steeper learning curve — overwhelming for beginners
  • Maps less polished than Explore 2
  • No touchscreen

Edge 130 Plus

The Lightweight Sidekick

Pros

  • Featherweight at just 33 g — barely notice it
  • Affordable — lowest price of the three
  • Very simple to use, minimal setup
  • Supports HRM, power meter, cadence
  • ClimbPro included (Plus version)
  • Incident detection & LiveTrack
  • Perfect second computer for race day

Cons

  • No onboard maps — navigation very limited
  • Smallest screen (1.8") — fewer data fields
  • Shortest battery life (12 h)
  • No Wi-Fi — must sync via Bluetooth
  • No Training Load or full performance metrics
  • No radar support
  • Limited Connect IQ support

Final Summary & Recommendations

Edge Explore 2 — Best for Touring & Exploration

If navigation is your top priority and you ride in unfamiliar places, the Explore 2 is unmatched. Its large touchscreen and full mapping make it ideal for bike-packing, cycle tourism, or any rider who wants a GPS device that feels like a proper map. Not the choice for data-driven training.

Edge 530 — Best for Serious Training & Racing

The 530 is Garmin's most well-rounded mid-range performance computer. If you follow structured training plans, track power zones, analyze TSS/NP, or race, this is the one. It has the deepest feature set and the best battery life. Also great for mountain bikers.

Edge 130 Plus — Best for Commuting & Simplicity

The 130 Plus is perfect when you want reliable ride tracking without the complexity. Commuters, casual riders, and those who just want speed/distance/power without menus will love it. Its tiny size means it fits any bike unobtrusively. Also an excellent race-day backup device.

Key takeaway: All three devices share the same core Garmin ecosystem — ANT+, Bluetooth, Garmin Connect sync, ClimbPro, and LiveTrack — so the choice comes down to what you value most.

Choose the Explore 2 if you prioritize maps and ease of navigation. Choose the Edge 530 if you prioritize training data, performance metrics, and battery. Choose the Edge 130 Plus if you prioritize simplicity, price, and weight.

The Edge 530 offers the best value for money if you only buy one device and plan to grow into it — it handles both navigation and training reasonably well, even if it's not the best at either individually.
